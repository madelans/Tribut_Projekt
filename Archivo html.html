IRENA SENDLER

The woman who saved a thousands of children lives in the war

Here´s a timeline of Irena Sendler´s life:

  • 1910-Born in Varsovia, Polonia.
  • 1939-She worked as a nurse in the Social welfare department when Germany invaded Poland.
  • 1940-She joined at the council for the helps jews when Germans created the first Ghetto in Poland.
  • During half one year she was with the children's family contact was offered to take them to salve them. In some cases, the family was opposed and she back to convinced, she found that they had all been taken to the train that would take them to the execution camp.
  • 1942-She gets rescue 2.500 children in many ways: from an ambulance where they faked diseases, until garbage basket, toolbox, potato pack, coffins. In her hands any object it became an escape route.
  • 1943-The Nazis learned of the activities where she was detained and taken to Pawiak prison and she was brutally tortured to obtain information about her collaborators and the children she was hiding.
  • She was sentenced to death, but a German soldier who was persuaded helped her escape by taking her for an “additional interrogation” and as soon they left, he yelled at her “run!” and the next day she got her own name the executed Poles.
  • 1965-She was awarded the title of Righteous Among the Nations and was named an honorary citizen of Israel.
  • 2003-She was awarded the title of Lady of the Order of the White Eagle, the highest civilian distinction in Poland.
  • 2008-She died at the age of 98.
